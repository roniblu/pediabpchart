2017 AAP classification reference

Children 1 to <13 y

  • Normal — < 90th percentile (SBP and DBP)
  • Elevated — ≥ 90th to < 95th, or 120/80 to < 95th (lower of)
  • Stage 1 HTN — ≥ 95th to < 95th + 12, or 130/80 to 139/89 (lower of)
  • Stage 2 HTN — ≥ 95th + 12, or ≥ 140/90 (lower of)

Adolescents ≥ 13 y

  • Normal — < 120/<80 mmHg
  • Elevated — 120/<80 to 129/<80 mmHg
  • Stage 1 HTN — 130/80 to 139/89 mmHg
  • Stage 2 HTN — ≥ 140/90 mmHg
Methodology & limitations

Height percentile uses the CDC 2000 stature-for-age LMS reference (ages 24–240 months), with linear interpolation between half-monthly LMS rows. The Z-score is computed via the Box–Cox formula Z = ((X/M)^L − 1) / (L·S).

Blood pressure percentile uses the 4th-order polynomial regression of age and height-Z published in the AAP 2017 guideline appendix (carried forward from the 2004 4th Report). The conditional mean BP for the patient's age, sex, and height-Z is computed, then the Z-score is Z = (BP − μ) / σ with the published σ (10.7128 / 10.4855 / 11.6032 / 10.9573 mmHg for SBP♂ / SBP♀ / DBP♂ / DBP♀). The Z-score is mapped through the standard normal CDF for the percentile.

Classification follows AAP 2017 Table 3. For children <13 y the percentile-based and absolute-mmHg cutoffs are evaluated and the lower (stricter) category is reported. Adolescents ≥13 y use the absolute adult thresholds.

For clinical screening only. This tool does not replace properly measured, repeated BP readings or clinical judgment. AAP guidance is to obtain ≥3 elevated readings on separate occasions before diagnosing hypertension. Children under 1 y use Task Force 1987 / neonatal references not implemented here.
